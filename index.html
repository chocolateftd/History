What are magic Bullets?

Magic bullets are chemicals which target and kill specific types of bacteria. These were first discovered in the early 20th century, and helped to save many lives. The idea for these grew from germ theory, and brought about one of the first treatments to have a phenomenal effect on peoples livelihood.

Timeline

1860s

Germ theory begins to develop

1870s

Koch discovers that you can dye bacteria to make it more easily visable under a microscope

1890

Paul Elrich is appointed as one of Kochs assistants, and begun studying germs and possible treatments

1900

Elrich suggested the possibility that you could use chemicals to target specific bacteria. He brought in hundreds of chemical substances and, along with his assistants, tried to find one of these chemicals.

1900-1907

Elrich made progress towards what he called ‘magic bullets’, discovering a drug to help treat trypanosomiasis.

1907

A previously discarded drug was retested on a rabbit with syphilis, finding it effective.

1900-Today

More treatments like this have been developed, and have influenced many more doctors in the development of medicine.

Paul Ehrlich

Paul Elrich played a major role in the development of magic bullets, and made major steps in their discovery. He was strongly influenced by Koch, who had previously discovered that you could dye bacteria to make them more easily visible under a microscope. Elrich knew this, and wondered that if chemicals could be used to dye microbes, could chemicals be used to kill them.
