Paul Ehrlich

Paul Erhlich played a major role in the development of magic bullets, and made major steps in their discovery. He was strongly influenced by Koch, who had previously discovered that you could dye bacteria to make them more easily visible under a microscope. Ehrlich knew this, and wondered that if chemicals could be used to dye microbes, could chemicals be used to kill them.

So, in 1900, he got together a team of his assistant, and tested hundreds of chemicals against bacteria. Over the course of 7 years, he made a few important discoveries, discovering the cure of Typanosomes, for instance. In 1907, a previously discarded chemical was given to rabbits with syphilis, proving effective – a major ‘magic bullet’.

While Ehrlich may only have discovered a few magic bullets, he opened many doors in medicine and allowed far more discoveries by other scientists. This work had a sway even today, and because of his work, doctors no longer treat patients for their symptoms, they treat them for the bacteria which causes the disease, saving many lives. Ehrlich greatly influenced modern medicine, and is a massively important figure in the history of medicine.
